Nordvale Dairy Cooperative Opens Third Processing Facility in Jutland

Nordvale Dairy Cooperative, a farmer-owned dairy processor headquartered in Aarhus, Denmark, opened its third processing facility this week following an 18-month construction programme. The new plant in southern Jutland will process milk from roughly 240 member farms and is expected to add about 90 permanent jobs to the region.

The cooperative, established in 1961, is owned by its 1,400 member farmers and produces butter, cheese, and milk powder primarily for the Danish and German retail markets. Managing director Kirsten Holmgaard said the expansion was funded through retained member earnings and a long-term facility loan from a Danish agricultural lender, with no outside equity investment.

"Our members have waited a long time for capacity closer to their farms," Holmgaard said at the opening. "Shorter collection routes mean fresher milk and lower fuel costs, and the savings go back to the people who own this business."

The facility has been certified to the cooperative's existing food-safety and environmental standards, and includes a heat-recovery system that the cooperative estimates will cut the plant's energy consumption by around 20 percent compared with its older sites. Nordvale reported steady revenue growth over the past three financial years and publishes annual results to its membership each March.

Local officials welcomed the investment. The regional business council noted that the cooperative has operated in the area for six decades without significant disruption and remains one of the larger private employers in the municipality.
