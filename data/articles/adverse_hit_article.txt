Dubai Court Records Reveal Money Laundering Charges Against Regional Finance Executive

A Dubai financial court has formally charged Farid Hassan Abadi, a Syrian national and regional coordinator for several Middle Eastern trading firms, with multiple counts of money laundering and sanctions evasion. The charges were unsealed on July 8, 2026.

According to the indictment, Abadi allegedly orchestrated a scheme to move approximately USD 9.2 million through a series of shell companies in Lebanon, Cyprus, and Turkey between 2023 and 2025. The funds are believed to have originated from entities subject to EU and US sanctions against Syria.

Prosecutors allege that Abadi used his position as a regional coordinator for Damascus Trading House LLC to establish front companies that disguised the origin of funds. Bank records submitted as evidence show wire transfers structured in amounts just below reporting thresholds, a technique known as "smurfing."

The Financial Action Task Force (FATF) grey-listing of the UAE in 2022, since resolved, had prompted enhanced due diligence requirements that ultimately led compliance teams at several banks to flag Abadi's transactions. Investigators also discovered that Abadi maintained multiple identity documents under variations of his name, including "F.H. Abadi" and "Farid Abadi."

Abadi's legal team has entered a not-guilty plea and argued that the transactions were legitimate commercial activities. A preliminary hearing is scheduled for September 2026.
